Not a Spotify clone

Spotify optimizes individual consumption. Dotify optimizes shared musical presence, artist sovereignty, and cultural transmission.

For listeners

Discover music through people, not only algorithms. Join a room and enter the same listening moment.

For artists

Publish through artist-owned SmartRuntimes with access policy, royalties, and protected audio references.

For communities

Turn isolated listening into a living cultural commons where music travels through human presence.

Production spine

Keys, access, and rooms are in place. The current focus: prove the trust flows with deterministic tests, and keep the experience simple enough to feel human.

01

Secure keys

Delivered. Pinata uploads and content-key delivery live behind a backend boundary; audio is encrypted server-side.

02

Unlock access

Delivered. Wallet-signed key requests with replay protection and on-chain access checks before full playback.

03

Stabilize rooms

Delivered. Hosted signaling with one-link joins, heartbeats, expiry, reconnect states, and honest preview metadata.

04

Test critical flows

In progress. Classic unlock, artist publish, and room join must be deterministic and CI-covered.

Design principles

Fail closed on access

Ambiguous access decisions deny playback. No silent fallbacks. The policy is the truth.

Invisible complexity

The user sees a shared link. The infrastructure handles keys, chains, and access policy underneath.

Artist sovereignty

Artists publish through runtimes they own. No platform intermediary controls the terms of access.

Repository memory

Context lives in docs/backlog/ and docs/context/. AI agents must read CLAUDE.md before implementing.

Dotify must be as simple as a shared link, as alive as a listening room, as fair as an auditable access policy, and as deep as a cultural commons.
