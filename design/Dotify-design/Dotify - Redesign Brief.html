Dotify · Art Direction & Implementation Brief

Music as living light.

The thing playing right now should light the whole room — its colour bleeds into the surfaces, and the first thing you see isn't a catalogue, it's the people already listening together. Web3 is felt as trust, never as bureaucracy.

01

What's holding the current UI back

Crypto-cyberpunk backdrop

The green warp-field lines read as exactly the generic web3/neon cliché the product wants to avoid. No warmth, no human presence, no sense of "living light".

A landing page on the product screen

The giant "Let the Music connect the dots" headline turns Home into marketing. The real catalogue is small and floats in a sea of navy.

Web3 reads as technical

Mono/terminal type for "Proof of Personhood DIM1" and "0.42 DOT" makes the chain feel like infrastructure instead of disappearing into trust.

Rooms feel administrative

The social core looks like an empty form — no faces, no presence, no "you're listening with someone". The village square is cold.

02

The design system — "Living Light"

Colour · album-aura engine

Keep the deep-blue heart. Every track carries an aura (two stops + an accent) that paints its generated cover and the ambient halos behind the whole UI. The world is lit by whatever's playing. Brand constants stay; Polkadot pink is used with restraint.

Type · one humanist voice

Hanken Grotesk throughout — soft, premium, warm — replacing the Space Grotesk / Inter / JetBrains-Mono trio. Display is the same family at heavy weight + tight tracking. Mono is retired from prices and access (numbers use tabular-nums), which alone de-crypts the feel.

Surface · light-catching glass

Panels are blurred glass with a 1px top highlight so they catch light from the aura, on radii of 14–28px. Generous spacing; soft, deep shadows. A toggle drops glass for flat surfaces when needed.

Motion · breathing, never busy

Aura drifts slowly; the room cover pulses while playing; reactions float. Visibility never depends on an entrance animation (transform-only) so print, reduced-motion and slow frames always show content. No infinite decorative loops on text.

Presence · the people, three ways

Avatars (portraits), glowing orbs, or initials-aura — chosen by tweak. Hosts get an accent ring; live equalizer bars mark who's actively listening. Rooms always show who is in them.

Web3 · quiet & warm

Trust marks (✦ Artist-owned Free for humans) and human microcopy: "The artist keeps the keys", "goes straight to the artist — no middle cut". DOT stays visible, framed as support, not speculation.

03

Screen by screen

Home / Discovery

Opens as a product, not a pitch. A live "X people listening together right now" header, a featured aura hero, then Happening now rooms surfaced before the catalogue, then Discover. A warm "Access, the human way" rail explains the model without jargon.

Shared listening room

Full-bleed in the track's light. Big glowing cover, presence cluster with host marked, synced transport (listeners "follow the host · in sync"), floating reactions, and a one-tap Copy link. Guests listen free — "only you need access to the track".

Create / share a room

One sheet titled "As easy as sharing a link": pick what's playing, see the live dotify.dot.li/r/CODE link, set a mood, open & copy. Zero wallet friction to start.

Wallet & unlock

Not-connected is an invitation ("Bring your wallet — keep your music"), passkey-first. Connected is a calm identity card with a "You hold your keys" note. Unlock is music-first: a small modal, the artist's name, and where the DOT goes — plus "keep the 42% preview".

05

Implementation in the existing React/Vite app

Files to modify

  • web/src/styles.css — swap tokens: warm base, glass surfaces, aura CSS vars (--aura-a/-b/-accent), retire mono for prices, add color-mix aura layers. Delete the warp-field rules.
  • components/AmbientCanvas.tsx & StarfieldCanvas.tsx — replace network/warp canvas with the CSS aura layer (cheaper, warmer).
  • views/ListenView.tsx — lead with live rooms, then catalogue.
  • views/RoomsView.tsx / PlayerView.tsx — adopt presence + full-bleed aura room.

Behaviour preserved

  • WebRTC host stream, Socket.IO rooms, host-based access — unchanged.
  • Classic pay → musicRoyPayAccess, 42% preview gating, personhood checks — same logic, warmer surface.
  • Wallet connect (passkey / extension) — same paths, calmer UI.
  • Aura is pure presentation: one CSS-var write on track-select drives the whole look.

Progressive rollout

Re-skin tokens & kill the warp-field

Land the new styles.css variables + glass; instant lift, no behaviour change.

Aura engine + Cover

Set --aura-* on track-select; introduce generated covers as fallback art.

06

Priorities & risks

Priority list

  • P0  Token re-skin + remove warp-field
  • P0  Rooms surfaced on Home + presence
  • P0  One-link room creation
  • P1  Aura engine + generated covers
  • P1  Warm wallet / unlock copy
  • P2  Lights-down mode, reactions, tweak variants
  • P2  Artist studio in the same language

UX risks to avoid

  • Over-saturating the aura until text loses contrast — keep the base floor + vignette.
  • Letting the wallet feel more important than the music.
  • Hiding critical errors or over-simplifying rights so access/ownership reads as a lie.
  • Forcing room guests to authenticate before they feel the room.
  • Decorative motion that loops forever or gates content visibility.
